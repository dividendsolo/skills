# Mental Model Catalog

The latticework the `munger` skill runs investment ideas against. Grouped by discipline, the way Munger built it - "you've got to have models from multiple disciplines."

**This is a living document.** It's seeded with the canonical models below; add, refine, and split entries over time. Each model should stay one line: **name - the one-sentence test it puts to an idea.** Keep it terse so the whole lattice fits in working memory during a run.

---

## Core filters (run every idea through these first)
- **Circle of competence** - Do I actually understand how this makes money, or am I pretending?
- **Durable moat** - Is the competitive advantage real, and widening or eroding?
- **Able & honest management** - How do they allocate capital; are incentives and candour aligned with owners?
- **Margin of safety** - Does the price assume optimism, or leave room to be wrong?

## Microeconomics & competition
- **Moats** - brand, switching costs, network effects, scale, patents/regulation: which one, how deep?
- **Competitive destruction** - what kills this business; is the industry a treadmill that competes away returns?
- **Economies of scale (and diseconomies)** - does size help or eventually bloat it?
- **Surfing / riding a wave** - is there a structural tailwind doing the heavy lifting?
- **Pricing power** - can it raise prices without losing customers?

## Math & probability
- **Compounding** - what does the return engine look like over time; reinvestment runway?
- **Expected value** - probability-weighted outcomes, not just the bull case.
- **Base rates** - how do businesses *like this one* usually fare? (outside view)
- **Margin of safety (quantitative)** - how wrong can I be and still not lose money?
- **Asymmetry** - is downside capped and upside open, or the reverse?

## Physics & engineering
- **Critical mass / threshold** - is there a tipping point where the business suddenly works (or breaks)?
- **Feedback loops** - does success compound itself (virtuous) or sow its own decline (vicious)?
- **Redundancy / single point of failure** - what one supplier, customer, or covenant could take the whole thing down?
- **Leverage (operating & financial)** - where is the fulcrum that magnifies results, both up and down?
- **Entropy / running to stand still** - how much capital must it burn just to hold its current position?
- **Reversion to the mean** - is today's margin or price an out-of-equilibrium state that will normalize?

## Biology & ecology
- **Ecological niche (specialist vs generalist)** - is it a narrow specialist (fragile when the world shifts) or an adaptable generalist?
- **Symbiosis vs parasitism** - does it create value with its suppliers and partners, or extract until it kills the host?
- **Carrying capacity** - how big can this market actually get, not how big the story wants it to be?
- **Adaptation** - when the environment changes, can it evolve, or is it about to be stranded?

## Psychology of human misjudgment (bias checklist - run against your *own* thesis)
- **Incentive-caused bias** - "show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome."
- **Commitment & consistency** - am I defending this because I already said I liked it?
- **Social proof** - am I in because everyone else is?
- **Authority / halo** - am I leaning on a famous holder or a charismatic CEO?
- **Deprival-superreaction (loss aversion / FOMO)** - am I chasing to avoid missing out?
- **Confirmation bias** - am I only collecting evidence that fits?
- **Recency / vividness** - is a recent story crowding out the long-run base rate?

## Inversion & general thinking
- **Invert, always invert** - how does this fail? What would make it a terrible investment?
- **Second-order effects** - "and then what?" beyond the obvious first move.
- **Opportunity cost** - measured against my best alternative, not against zero.
- **Lollapalooza** - are several forces stacking in the same direction (for or against)?
- **Occam / too-hard pile** - if it needs a convoluted story, is it just too hard?

## Temperament & Munger maxims
- **Quality over cheapness** - a great business at a fair price beats a fair business at a great price; am I buying quality or just something cheap?
- **The money is in the waiting** - does this thesis pay me to sit still, and am I actually willing to?
- **Avoid ruin first** - does any plausible outcome risk permanent loss of capital? Never bet the farm.
